City Development

City Development is a problem-solving game that aims to help players develop and improve their systems thinking skills. Players take on the roles of different systems within a city and work towards the development of a realistic urban environment. The game presents various types of systems, including transportation departments, power utilities, city security departments, schools, hospitals, and more. Each system has unique characteristics and functions, and players need to understand how these systems interact and develop, while solving various challenges.The game presents a series of problems and challenges, and players need to apply the principles and methods of systems thinking to find the best solutions. They can optimize the structure of systems, improve resource allocation, and streamline processes to solve problems and enhance the efficiency and sustainability of the city's systems. Through collaboration and cooperation, players can collectively face complex system challenges, share experiences and insights, and work together to find the best solutions.

Benefits

Players need to consider the roles of different systems based on real-life situations. This game helps players understand the theory and practice of systems thinking. Players continuously accumulate experience and knowledge, improving their systems thinking abilities and gaining a sense of achievement through problem-solving. By considering the development of the city from the perspective of various systems and viewing problems as a whole, the game helps players develop a holistic mindset and consider issues from multiple angles rather than in a one-sided manner.

In the process of city development, players are provided with moral dilemmas through their inclinations towards different systems. The development process involves trade-offs and decision-making. The game presents scenarios and challenges that require players to make decisions in situations with limited resources and uncertainty, testing their moral principles and improving their decision-making and judgment skills.

Every decision in city development is crucial, and systems thinking helps players uncover the underlying causes of problems and find innovative solutions. The diverse development options stimulate players' creativity and innovative thinking. At the same time, systems thinking emphasizes the importance of overall coordination. The game can foster a sense of teamwork and coordination by promoting collaboration among players, helping them understand the importance of synergy, interdependence, and shared goals, and cultivating teamwork awareness and coordination skills.

Preparation

City Development utilizes role-playing to illustrate the interactions of different systems, allowing players to consider which aspects should be prioritized in urban development. Players can contribute to the development of the city from the perspectives of different systems. The facilitator should prepare system cards for the game.

Game Manual:

Game Overview:

City Development is a collaborative game where multiple players work together to develop a city.

Game Objectives:

The objectives of the game are to increase the city's population and maintain a stable city base score. Players need to coordinate and collaborate with various departments to develop the city, respond to emergencies, and keep the city in a healthy and sustainable state of development.

Game Rules:

  • The city starts with a base score of 100 and a population of 50,000. The initial city wealth is 1 million.
  • The city is composed of eight systems. Each round, three systems are chosen for discussion and upgrade.
  • If there is insufficient city infrastructure to serve the population, the city's score will be deducted by 10, and the population will decrease by 50,000 for that round.
  • In addition to the given development opportunities, players can use their city wealth once per round to develop a specific system. Developing a school requires 2 million, developing the city itself requires 4 million, and developing other systems requires 1 million.
  • The game consists of 25 rounds, or the game ends if the city score reaches 0.
  • Starting from the fifth round, a sudden event will occur every two rounds. If the city cannot cope with the event, the city score will be deducted by 10. If the event is successfully resolved, the score will be increased by 10.
  • If the event cannot be resolved successfully, players can choose whether to inform the public. If they choose to inform, the city population will decrease by 50,000, but the city score will be restored by 5.
  • The game ends with a final score based on the city's population, city score, and city wealth. Every additional 10,000 city residents or 1 million cumulative city wealth earns 1 point.

Role/Player Guide:

(Changes to the city's population capacity)

  • Transportation Department: Provides transportation services, etc. Level 1: 10,000 people, increases by 50,000 for each level. After level 5, each level increases by 50,000.
  • Water Supply Department: Supplies water resources, etc. Level 1: 100,000 people, increases by 100,000 for each level.
  • Power Utility: Provides power services, etc. Level 1: 10,000 people, increases by 50,000 for each level.
  • Environmental Protection Department: Pollution control, waste recycling, etc. Level 1: 150,000 people, increases by 50,000 for each level.
  • School: Provides educational services, enhances surrounding development, etc. Level 1: 10,000 people, increases by 50,000 for each level. Income increases by 1 million and city population increase 50000.
  • Hospital: Treats patients, etc. Level 1: 10,000 people, increases by 50,000 for each level.
  • City Security Department: Ensures public safety, police and fire protection, etc. Level 1: 10,000 people, increases by 100,000 for each level. After level 5, each level increases by 50,000.
  • Commercial Development: Commercial and trade-related construction. Level 1: 10,000 people, increases by 50,000 for each level. Income increases by 2 million and city population increase 50000.

Sudden Event

  • Holiday: Increased demand for transportation by 50,000, water supply by 50,000, power supply by 100,000, environmental protection by 50,000, city security by 50,000, and commercial development by 50,000.
  • Natural Disaster: Increased demand for transportation by 100,000, water supply by 50,000, power supply by 100,000, environmental protection by 100,000, city security by 100,000, and hospital by 100,000.
  • Flood: Increased demand for transportation by 50,000, water supply by 100,000, environmental protection by 50,000, city security by 100,000, and hospital by 50,000.
  • Fire: Increased demand for transportation by 50,000, water supply by 100,000, city security by 100,000, hospital by 100,000, and environmental protection by 100,000.
  • Public Health Emergency: Increased demand for hospital by 150,000, transportation by 50,000, city security by 50,000, water supply by 50,000, and power supply by 50,000.
  • Social Security Incident: Increased demand for city security by 100,000 and hospital by 50,000.
  • Traffic Accident: Increased demand for transportation by 100,000 and hospital by 50,000.
  • Sudden Water Interruption: Increased demand for water supply by 100,000 and city security by 50,000.
  • Environmental Pollution Incident: Increased demand for environmental protection by 100,000 and hospital by 50,000.
  • Major Events, Concerts, Sports Events: Increased demand for transportation by 100,000, water supply by 50,000, power supply by 50,000, and city security by 50,000.

Game Mechanics:

The rational upgrade of different systems increases the city's population. It is important to note that different system upgrades have different effects. Plan the upgrade sequence and priorities wisely.

Game Tips and Strategies:

Avoid blindly upgrading and instead strategically allocate the number of upgrades and city wealth each round. Avoid excessive development and consider how to respond to sudden events.

During the Competition:

During this competition, players will go through the following steps:

  • Select system types.
  • Discuss their functions and the overall direction of the game with other players.
  • Discuss and decide which systems to develop in each round as a group.
  • Respond to sudden events.
  • Optimize the development direction.

Discussion

The host asks each player to reflect on any shortcomings in their decisions.

Finally, players have the option to reset a round, make improvements, and then choose to continue the game to see if the results differ.
